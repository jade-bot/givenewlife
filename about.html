We believe that women can effect positive change on the mothering culture of their own countries.

Women around the world often live suppressed lives, voiceless and falling prey to the cycles of poverty, disease and death. New Life exists to empower these women with vital educational support, nutritional aid, and health care access to help them live a New Life. We are currently focusing on three slum areas in the Ethiopian capital city of Addis Ababa.

New Life's emphasis begins with a Meal Center to offer a nutritious meal once a day to a group of families who will eventually be the core leaders and promoters at New Life. We are researching the best ways to feed these vulnerable populations with the nutrition needed to heal and stay healthy. Access to clean water is a major thrust of this first-line defense.

In conjunction with the Meal Center, breastfeeding education and support is already beginning. We are training mothers who have successfully breastfed to become peer counselors to other mothers to help solve common roadblocks, dispel myths and encourage longer duration of breastfeeding.

In Phase 2 of New Life's operations, we will be addressing safer birthing options, improving sanitary living conditions and starting a supplemental food pantry.

Phase 3 includes plans for a donor breast milk bank for vulnerable infants, vocational training for adults and supporting education for children.

Our Operative Principles

Once a family comes into the program, we serve them until they no longer need assistance. We work to help them thrive in any areas of need.

We believe that there will be enough. We have set our target population parameters, but when we come across people outside of our target population who need assistance, we are glad to help.

We are conduits. We expect that the assistance we provide will trickle out into the community as people share their new knowledge and help others. By helping the people who come our way, we are giving them the tools to help others they encounter. In this way, we will empower societal change, as best practices become the norm.
